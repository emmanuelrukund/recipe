Classic Guacamole

By Melat Berhe

Guacamole is a classic Mexican dish and condiment made primarily from ripe avocados mashed to a smooth consistency known for its versatility and is commonly served as a dip with tortilla chips, a topping for tacos, nachos, or salads, or as a side dish alongside various Mexican and Tex-Mex cuisines.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1 lime (juiced)
  • 1/4 cup diced onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 jalapeno pepper (seeded and minced)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder (optional)
  • 1/2 tomato (diced) - optional for added texture and flavor

PROCEDURE:

  1. Cut the avocados in half, remove the pits, and scoop out the flesh into a bowl.
  2. Mash the avocado with a fork or potato masher until you reach your desired consistency. Some people prefer chunky guacamole, while others like it smooth.
  3. Add the lime juice to the mashed avocado and mix well. The lime juice will help prevent the avocados from oxidizing and turning brown.
  4. Add the diced onion, chopped cilantro, minced jalapeno pepper, salt, black pepper, ground cumin (optional), and garlic powder (optional) to the bowl. Mix everything together until well combined.
  5. If desired, add diced tomato to the guacamole mixture and gently fold it in. The tomato adds extra texture and flavor to the guacamole.
  6. Taste the guacamole and adjust the seasoning if needed. Add more salt, lime juice, or other seasonings according to your preference.
  7. Serve the guacamole immediately or cover it with plastic wrap, pressing the plastic wrap directly onto the surface of the guacamole to prevent air exposure and browning. Refrigerate until ready to serve.
